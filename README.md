It's 2026, and people get kind of  b0rked by AI.

... so I am now trying to automate this effort by means of talking to AI chat-bots. Here is my attempt. I am using a prompt prefix, as a starting point (you cant set system prompts with google AI mode)

One of the strong point is that you can ask the AI to create illustrations of concepts, and that you can use these illustrations in your lesson.

I think the main point is to teach your child how to ask questions. This style of creating lessons requires from you, who is building the lessons to ask questions too. I think that is my main criteria for success, if I can teach the importance of asking questions.


Here is an example for the type of initial prompts used to create this material. I then use that as a starting point for continuing the dialogue. I am using the result as blueprint for my lesson.
```
You are explaining popular science topics for children.
Explain the main point of the topic clearly, by means of analogies, and using a basic English vocabulary.
You explain, how scientists reached an understanding of the topic, and what lead to these insights.
You use examples to explain the topic, and provide illustrations for the concepts and its mechanism.
Explain complex topics from the top down, vividly, in the same manner.
                                                                               
Explain the origin of continental drift and plate tectonics and continental drift.
```

funny thing: Language models have one socially acceptable occupation - programming. Everything else they produce is frowned upon, for whatever reasons and regarded as AI slop. However I do value these results, as they are the result of a dialogue - and Socrates and Plato both liked that method of teaching about things...

What I observed about some of the vibe coders in the wild: some of them don't have a clue about how program is working. As if everyone has been promoted to the role of senior manager: they know the desired results, but are not familiar with the details of how the program is working, sometimes therefore do not know the format or mechanism of how data is being stored persistently. I am very afraid of reaching such a state of affairs with my 'vibe learning/vibe teaching' effort.

Still, I am only partially relying on the output of the language model: I still have some clue (hopefully), and I am just using the language model in order to flesh out the details of the lesson plan. Maybe that's how it should be...
